Case Study – Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. These attacks often happen via emails, messages, or fake websites that look trustworthy.
A typical phishing attack follows these steps:

The attacker sends a message or email that appears to be from a trusted source, such as a bank or social media platform.
The message urges the victim to click on a link or download an attachment, often using urgent language like “Your account will be blocked.”
When the victim clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website designed to capture login credentials.
The victim enters their details, which are then sent directly to the attacker.
The attacker uses this information to steal money, hack accounts, or commit identity theft.

Students and young professionals are often targeted because they are active online and may be less cautious about verifying messages. The consequences of phishing attacks can include financial loss, unauthorized access to personal accounts, data theft, and stress or embarrassment for the victim.

Awareness and caution are key to prevention. Users should verify email sources, avoid clicking suspicious links, and use multi-factor authentication to protect their accounts and also should report this.
